# OPFOR Supply — DESIGN.md

The single source of truth for how OPFOR Supply presents itself. It captures the visual
system, the narrative arc, the positioning and moat, the offer, and the voice rules — so that
every sales artifact (the proposal PDF that already exists, and the sales-call deck built from
this doc) reads as one product from one company.

---

## 1. Purpose

**OPFOR Supply** is clinical supply demand forecasting and scenario simulation for multi-year,
global trials. This document exists so that anyone — a person or an AI — building a sales asset
can produce something that is visually, narratively, and tonally identical to the proposal the
client already received.

Two things are downstream of this doc and must stay aligned with it:

1. **The proposal PDF** — a pixel-faithful, Swiss-Atlas document generated by the app
   (`src/components/proposal/`). This is what the client reads and forwards to legal/procurement.
2. **The sales-call deck** — the PowerPoint you present live. Section 7 maps the proposal arc
   to a slide-by-slide deck.

Non-negotiable framing rules that apply to *both* assets:

- The brand is written **"OPFOR Supply"**, never "OPFOR.ai" and never "OPFOR AI." Compliance and
  legal audiences fixate on the letters "AI"; we give them nothing to fixate on.
- Every claim is **status-gated**. Shipped features are stated flatly. Vision features are marked
  "in build" and never presented as live. See Section 4.
- **Never name a competitor.** Not in the doc, not on a slide, not out loud.
- **Never surface patient-level, blinded, or treatment-arm data.** The architecture prevents it;
  the messaging reinforces it.

## 2. Brand & visual system

### 2.1 The language: "Swiss Atlas"

Swiss timetable typography meets cartography. Think of a train departure board crossed with a
survey plate: hairline rules, oversized display numerals, monospace instrument labels, generous
paper margins, and a single red-ink orthographic globe as the one piece of imagery. It reads as
precise, calm, and engineered — the opposite of a gradient-heavy SaaS pitch. Restraint is the
brand. Red is a scalpel, not a highlighter.

### 2.2 Color

Brand color is **red `#E5192B`**, used as an **accent only** — rule bars, the CTA period, the
struck-through "FREE" on the value stack, the logo, and the highlighted words in the capabilities
strip. **Red is never body text.** Everything else is ink on paper (light) or slate-white on a
near-black ground (dark).

The proposal document reads *all* color through a single scoped token contract (`--pd-*` on
`.proposal-doc` in `src/styles/globals.css`). Light values are the base; the dark variant is a
scoped override that swaps the same token names. Any element built on these tokens is
automatically dual-mode.

| Token | Role | Light | Dark |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--pd-paper` | page ground | `#F7F8FA` | `#0B0F17` |
| `--pd-ink` | headlines / primary text | `#0E1420` | `#E7ECF4` |
| `--pd-tag` | lead / body copy | `rgba(14,20,32,.72)` | `rgba(231,236,244,.72)` |
| `--pd-dim` | meta / labels | `rgba(14,20,32,.56)` | `rgba(231,236,244,.56)` |
| `--pd-mid` | tertiary (opaque, sits over the globe) | `#9EA2A8` | `#5E636C` |
| `--pd-faint` | ghost numerals (phase/step numbers) | `rgba(14,20,32,.12)` | `rgba(231,236,244,.12)` |
| `--pd-line` | hairline rules | `rgba(14,20,32,.16)` | `rgba(255,255,255,.10)` |
| `--pd-line-strong` | emphasized / section-head rules | `rgba(14,20,32,.5)` | `rgba(255,255,255,.34)` |
| `--pd-chip` | client-logo backdrop | `rgba(14,20,32,.05)` | `rgba(255,255,255,.07)` |
| red accent | rule bars, CTA period, "FREE", logo | `#E5192B` | `#E5192B` (unchanged) |

App-shell tokens (the editor/dashboard, a separate light-only Apple-esque theme) live in
`tokens.css`: `--red: #E5192B`, `--red-hover: #c30f1f`, `--red-soft: rgba(229,25,43,.13)`. The
deck should follow the **proposal document** palette above, not the app shell.

Note for the deck: **paint the ground on each slide, not on a master background.** (In the PDF
this is a Chromium print quirk — a tall ancestor's background drops after page 1 — but the same
per-page discipline keeps a deck consistent.)

### 2.3 Typography

Five faces, each with one job. Do not improvise new roles.

| Face | Role | Where |
|---|---|---|
| **Space Grotesk** | display: headlines, section titles, oversized numerals, stats | `.pd-display`, all `h1`/`h2`/big numbers |
| **Inter** | body copy | set on `.proposal-doc` |
| **IBM Plex Mono** | instrument labels: section indices (`01 / 10`), meta, folios, tags, price readouts | `.pd-meta` (10.5px, uppercase, `.08em` tracking, tabular nums) |
| **OCR-A Extended** | cover tagline, folios, section indices — the "instrument" accent | `.pd-tag`, `.pd-ocr` |
| **Fraunces** (serif) | serif variant only — section titles when `data-variant="serif"` | opt-in |

Signature type moves: headlines are set **uppercase, ~64px, `leading-[0.9]`,
`tracking-[-0.045em]`** with a dimmed second line (`--pd-mid`). Section heads pair a mono
`NN / 10` index with a 34px Space Grotesk title. Phase/step numbers are oversized *faint* Space
Grotesk numerals (`--pd-faint`) sitting behind the copy.

### 2.4 The globe motif

A **red-ink orthographic globe** — a wireframe hemisphere, cartographic, thin-lined. It sits
subtly behind the right half of the cover; headline text may overlap it. It is the only image in
the entire system. Variants control how it is framed (bleeding to the edge, or clipped inside a
survey-plate frame with a small corner globe peeking on continuation pages). On the deck, the
globe is the hero visual for the cover and the closing slide; elsewhere it stays a faint
backdrop, never a decorative sticker.

### 2.5 Structural signatures (reusable on slides)

- **`.pd-shead`** — a strong top hairline with a **56px × 3px red bar** at the left edge (32px
  for sub-blocks). This is the single most recognizable OPFOR mark. Every section starts with it.
- **Mono section index** — `01 / 10`, `07 / 10`, set in OCR-A/Plex Mono, top-left of each head.
- **Folio footer** — a mono instrument strip on every page (client name, confidential, page).
- **Ghost numerals** — huge faint Space Grotesk phase/step numbers.
- **Capabilities strip** — a bordered mono row of short readouts with select words in red
  (`Demand per protocol`, `Monte Carlo simulations`, `Live reforecast`, `IRT actuals`, …).

### 2.6 Logo

One wordmark everywhere: `OPFOR_LOGO_NEW_CROPPED.png` (flat crop), used on both light and dark
grounds. The glow variant is retired. Do not recolor, stretch, or box the wordmark. Red only
appears in the logo and the accents named above.

### 2.7 Variants (for reference)

`data-variant` on the document, previewable at `/preview?v=<name>`: `atlas` (default) ·
`instrument` (red instrument labels) · `plate` (framed survey-plate pages) · `poster` (uppercase
700 titles) · `serif` (Fraunces titles) · `dark` · `globe` · `dark-globe` · `plate-globe` ·
`plate-globe-dark`. The editor ships **Light = `plate-globe`** and **Dark = `plate-globe-dark`**
as the two default export editions. Dark-mode exception: the services-agreement/signature pages
always stay on paper, because clients e-sign them.

## 3. The narrative arc

The proposal is a **10-section story** (moat edition; 9 without the Data Boundaries page). Order,
job, emotional beat, and the real copy for each:

**Cover** — *Establish authority and scope; no numbers yet.*
Oversized uppercase client name over `Clinical Supply Forecasting Simulator` in dimmed ink, the
red-ink globe behind the right half, a stamp line (`Proposal 928801 V1.0 12 JUL 2026`), and the
OCR-A tagline. The subtitle: *"A purpose-built demand forecasting and scenario-planning engine
that grows with your team, and your clinical programs. Unlimited scenarios, studies, & seats, for
life."* — with **"for life"** in red. Beat: this is serious, and it is built for you.
Deliberately no price or timeline here — the cost reveal is saved for Section 07/08.

**01 · Areas of opportunity** — *Name the pain in their words.* (Say line: "Where accuracy and
speed are being capped today.")
Intro: *"Forecasting is innately high-leverage. Done well it compounds in your favor; done
manually it compounds against you."* Four numbered opportunities: spreadsheets that break on any
assumption change; deterministic planning that can't express uncertainty; scenario planning that
takes days of manual rework; no single source of truth across supply, clinical, and finance. Beat:
recognition — "they've seen my workflow."

**02 · Your solution** — *Resolve each pain, symmetrically.* (Say line: "Clean inputs. Honest
uncertainty. Your process.")
Intro: *"These gaps close the same way every time. Clean inputs, a model that shows its
uncertainty, and a process your team owns."* Four benefits mapping 1:1 to the four opportunities:
instant recompute on any assumption change; Monte Carlo risk at your target service level;
one-click scenarios; a shared, auditable dashboard giving one aligned number in demand and
dollars. Beat: relief — "there's a clean way through."

**03 · What ships** — *Make it concrete and finite.* (Say line: "A working system, not a slide
deck.")
Six deliverables: the calibrated forecasting model; the Monte Carlo risk engine; the interactive
scenario dashboard; a clean intake for reports you already export; documentation so your team
runs it without us; handover training + 30 days of post-launch support. Beat: tangibility.

**04 · How we get there / the timeline** — *De-risk the build.* (Title: "N weeks, week by week";
say: "A phased build. The timetable holds.")
Four one-week phases, each with a tagline, what happens, why it matters, and deliverable tags:
(1) Kickoff & Discovery — "map the terrain before you build"; (2) Model Build — "calibrated to
you"; (3) Dashboard & Scenarios — "one-click answers for the room"; (4) Signal & Warm Handoff —
"proof it holds, then a clean handoff" (run against known outcomes, then documentation, training,
30-day support). Beat: control — the timetable holds.

**05 · The engagement** — *Show who does the work.* (Say line: "Senior team. Narrow focus.")
A small senior team: Engagement Lead (CORE, full-time, single point of contact) and Forecasting &
Data Systems (CORE, builds the model + Monte Carlo engine and integrates it). Beat: no
account-manager-to-engineer relay; the person who scopes it builds it.

**06 · Why OPFOR** — *Earn belief.* (Say line: "Modeling depth. Your process.")
Three big stats — **6+ yrs** applying AI to supply and forecasting, **95%+** target service levels
engineered into every model, **End-to-end** from first report to boardroom dashboard — then a
blurb, a two-column numbered reason grid (the **moat-backed** points when moat is on; see
Section 4), and the bordered capabilities strip. Beat: "these people are the real thing."

**07 · Data boundaries** (moat edition only) — *Disarm compliance before they ask.* (Say line:
"What we hold. What we never touch.")
Opens: *"OPFOR is planning and decision support. The boundary below is architectural, not
policy."* Four claims — **Zero access to your systems** · **Your protocol never enters the
product** · **Counts, not people** · **No treatment-arm data, in any mode** — then the framed
close: **"Out of GxP scope by design … Adoption does not wait on a computerized-system validation
project."** Beat: the objection dissolves. This is the page that gets the deal past legal.

**08 · The investment** — *Reveal price against value.* (Say line, moat: "Seconds to an answer. No
vendor queue." / base: "Year one, then half. Locked for life.")
Clean ruled price rows, the two-tier year-one/renewal block with the renewal drop called out in
red, then the value stack — every bonus with its real dollar value and an Included/credited stamp,
closed by the **struck-through total in red beside a red "FREE."** Payment strip:
*"50% at signature · balance across Q3 and Q4 · renewals billed annually in advance · Net 30."*
Beat: the price looks small next to the stack. Full offer in Section 5.

**09 · Next steps** — *Make saying yes trivial.* (Say line: "Three moves. Then we build.")
Three numbered steps (approve to reserve the build slot; kickoff call to grant data access and
confirm assumptions; we begin within one week of kickoff), the **guarantee** framed in a
red-topped instrument block — *"If the delivered model does not hold to the agreed service-level
target on validation data, we keep working at no additional cost until it does."* — and the
oversized CTA close: **"See it run. Supply the world."** with red periods, over
`info@opforsupply.com · opforsupply.com`. Beat: momentum.

**10 · Services agreement** — *Remove friction from procurement.*
Generic-but-complete MSA boilerplate (Services, Fees, Invoicing, Payment Terms, IP, Confidentiality,
Indemnity, Limitation of Liability, General). Always rendered on **paper**, even in dark editions,
because the client e-signs it. This spans two pages by design. Not deck material — it exists so
the PDF is turnkey for legal.

## 4. Positioning & moat

These are the deck's core talking points. Format: **claim → why it's true → how to say it.** All
are **usable now** unless marked VISION.

**A scenario in seconds, not a consultant's queue.**
Why: the deterministic engine runs in the client's own browser; any assumption change
re-forecasts on the spot. How to say it: *"An assumption change becomes an answer in seconds, in
your browser. Self-serve, unlimited, no analyst queue."*

**IRT-agnostic by architecture.**
Why: OPFOR is a neutral forecasting layer over whatever randomization system a study runs.
Medidata is live today; other formats onboard per study. How to say it: *"A neutral layer over
whatever randomization system each study runs. Medidata live today; other formats onboard per
study."* **Do NOT use the phrase "Switzerland of IRT."**

**Zero access to your systems.**
Why: no OPFOR account, credential, or permission exists in the client's IRT or any validated
system; the client uploads reports they already receive. How to say it: *"Zero OPFOR accounts,
credentials, or permissions exist in your IRT or any validated system … That is the entire data
path."* Selling point: **nothing to vendor-qualify.**

**Your protocol never enters the product.**
Why: no document upload; setup consumes planning parameters only (countries, visit schedule, kit
types). How to say it: *"There is no document upload. Setup consumes planning parameters only."*

**No patient data. Ever.**
Why: the subject report is reduced to per-site, per-month counts *in the browser*; only counts
leave the machine; no subject-level records, no treatment arms. How to say it: *"No subject-level
records, no treatment arms. Enrollment reduces to per-site counts in your browser. Only the counts
leave your machine."*

**No treatment-arm data, in any mode.**
Why: the parser never reads arm, stratum, or randomization columns; the forecast runs on kit-type
demand. How to say it: *"Blinding stays intact by construction, not by policy."*

**Out of GxP scope by design.**
Why: OPFOR is not a system of record and performs no GxP activity; it ingests decision-support
exports only. How to say it: *"Out of GxP scope by design … Adoption does not wait on a
computerized-system validation project."* Use **"out of GxP scope by design"** and
**"validation-ready."** **NEVER** say "GxP compliant," "GxP validated," or "validated system."

**Every forecast is reproducible.**
Why: the engine is deterministic; any forecast re-derives exactly from its config snapshot, and
every change is versioned. How to say it: *"Deterministic engine: any forecast re-derives exactly
from its configuration snapshot. Every change is versioned."*

**Calibrated to your data, not a benchmark.**
Why: every model ships trained against the client's actual historical and protocol data. How to
say it: *"Trained against your actual historical and protocol data, not a generic industry
template."*

**Decision-ready, or we say so.**
Why: honesty as a feature. How to say it: *"If the data can't support a confident forecast at your
target service level, we tell you before you pay for a model that can't deliver it."*

**Transparent, published pricing.**
Why vs. the category: opaque enterprise vendors quote by RFP. How to say it: state the price
plainly, on the slide. The transparency *is* the differentiator — don't editorialize it.

**VISION — the what-if copilot (in build, never shipped).**
Ask in plain English; the engine reruns the forecast and answers in seconds. Always phrase as
**"in build"** or **"on the roadmap."** Never demo it, never imply it exists today.

### Hard rules (memorize)

- Never name competitors (no N-SIDE, Signant, Suvoda, or any other, anywhere).
- Never present a vision feature as shipped. The copilot is the only vision item; keep it labeled.
- Never surface patient / blinded / treatment-arm data, in any asset or any live demo.
- Never say "AI-compliant," "GxP compliant/validated," or lean on "AI" toward a compliance
  audience. The product is "OPFOR Supply."

## 5. The offer (Structure B)

### 5.1 Pricing — two tiers, one locked forever

- **Year one:** e.g. **$68,000** (set per client in the editor; the default cost item ships at $0
  so you price it live).
- **Annual renewal (year two onward):** **$34,000 — half of year one — locked for life.** The
  proposal literally prints *"Half of year one, locked for life."* in red, and the renewal drop is
  the number the eye lands on.
- The renewal is stated as *"your annual price never increases."*

### 5.2 Payment terms

**50% at signature · balance across Q3 and Q4 of year one · renewals billed annually in advance ·
Net 30.** (Codified in the agreement: 50% on execution, remainder in two equal installments in Q3
and Q4; renewal invoiced in full in advance each year; 30-day terms.)

Headliners to lead with on the deck: **Unlimited studies / seats / scenarios, for life** ·
**Locked-for-life renewal** · **white-glove onboarding + do-it-for-you study builds** ·
**weekly working call** · **two RTSM/IRT integration fee waivers (credited on renewal)** ·
**SOC 2-compliant host-for-you.** The blank-value "for life" items get a real qty baked in on the

### 5.4 Deal-closing pivots

- **Optional year-one % discount** — a single lever to close in the room. When set, it prints in
  both percent and dollars (e.g. "15% off · save $10,200") and reduces only the year-one price;
  the renewal stays locked. Leave it blank and it never shows.
- **Optional IRT integration add-on** — billed **per integration** (default $5,680), shown with
  its price but **excluded from the year-one total**, and **refunded onto the following-year
  renewal.** It reads as "Optional" in the price table.

### 5.5 The psychology

Front-load year one, then make the renewal a rubber-stamp: at **$34K**, the renewal is *half* the
entry price and the value stack (**~$66,626**) dwarfs it roughly **2:1**. The buyer signs a real
year-one number once, and every year after is an easy internal yes against a price that can never
rise. The value stack does the persuasion; the price line just has to look small beside it.

## 6. Voice & tone

Spartan, laconic, declarative. Second person ("your," "you"). Calm authority — the tone of an
instrument readout, not a pitch.

Rules:

- **No em dashes anywhere.** Use a period or a comma.
- No marketing adjectives, no exclamation points, no hype.
- No competitor names.
- No "AI" scare-language toward compliance audiences.
- Never expose patient / blinded / treatment-arm data.
- Ground every claim in fact. Vision stays labeled as vision.

Rewrite examples:

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| "Our revolutionary AI platform delivers game-changing accuracy!" | "The model recomputes instantly when any assumption changes." |
| "Fully GxP-compliant and validated for regulated environments — trusted by industry leaders." | "Out of GxP scope by design. Adoption does not wait on a computerized-system validation project." |
| "Run scenarios fast — way faster than legacy consultants and clunky old IRT tools." | "An assumption change becomes an answer in seconds, in your browser. No analyst queue." |
| "Ask it anything in plain English and get instant answers!" | "A plain-English what-if copilot is in build." |

## 7. Translating this to a sales deck

A **12-slide** deck that maps the proposal arc. Keep one idea per slide, the `.pd-shead` red bar
and mono index on every slide, Space Grotesk headlines, and the paper/dark ground consistent
throughout. Everything below is drawn from Sections 2–6 — do not invent capabilities.

| # | Slide | Content | Visual treatment | Beat |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Cover** | Client name (huge, uppercase) over "Clinical Supply Forecasting Simulator"; tagline with "for life" in red; stamp line | **Globe** hero behind the right half; OCR-A tagline | Authority |
| 2 | **Areas of opportunity** | The 4 numbered pains, verbatim | Numbered rows, red index bar; no imagery | Recognition |
| 3 | **Your solution** | The 4 benefits mapped 1:1 to slide 2 | Two-column pain→fix, or 4 numbered rows | Relief |
| 4 | **What ships** | The 6 deliverables | Clean ruled list; "A working system, not a slide deck." | Tangibility |
| 5 | **The plan** | 4 one-week phases with taglines | Ghost numerals 01–04, week tags; "The timetable holds." | Control |
| 6 | **The engagement** | Senior 2-person CORE team, no relay | Two cards, CORE badges | Trust |
| 7 | **Why OPFOR** | 3 big stats (6+ yrs / 95%+ / End-to-end) + capabilities strip | **Big-stat** row; bordered mono capabilities strip with red words | Belief |
| 8 | **The moat** | 3–4 headline moat claims (seconds-not-queue, IRT-agnostic, reproducible, calibrated) | 2-col claim grid; one **big stat** ("seconds") | Differentiation |
| 9 | **Data boundaries** | Zero access · protocol never enters · counts not people · no arm data · **out of GxP scope by design** | Architectural 4-claim grid + framed GxP close | Objection dissolved |
| 10 | **The investment** | Two-tier price: Year one vs. **half, locked for life**; renewal drop in red | **Two-tier price** block, renewal called out in red | Value reveal |
| 11 | **The bonuses** | The value stack; **$66,626 struck through → red FREE**; payment terms strip | **Value-stack** rows with values + stamps; struck total | "It's tiny next to this" |

Optional 13th/14th slides if the room warrants: a **VISION** slide for the what-if copilot
(labeled "in build"), and/or a **discount pivot** slide held in reserve to close live.

**What NOT to put on any slide:**

- **No competitor names.** Not even implied ("unlike legacy IRT vendors" is a competitor
  reference — cut it).
- **No vision-as-shipped.** The what-if copilot is "in build," always, or it's off the deck.
- **No patient / blinded / treatment-arm data**, and no screenshot that could show any. Counts
  only.
- **No "GxP compliant/validated," no "AI-compliant."** Use "out of GxP scope by design" /
  "validation-ready."
- **No em dashes**, no exclamation points, no marketing adjectives.
- **No red as body text.** Red is the bar, the period, the "FREE," the logo — nothing else.
- **No price before slide 10.** The reveal earns its value context first.

Build the deck on the **proposal document palette** (Section 2.2), the five faces (Section 2.3),
and the structural signatures (Section 2.5). If a slide could be dropped into the PDF and not look
out of place, it is on-brand.
